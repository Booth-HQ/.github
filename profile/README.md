## When the crowd peaks, everyone goes blind

At a packed event, mobile data dies under load and nobody installs an app for one night. Attendees cannot get answers, and organizers have no real-time read on what is confusing the crowd while it is still happening. Today that cost hides inside signage, staff, and the info-desk queue.

## A phone call works where data does not

Dial a local number. Ask anything. Get an answer in 40+ languages. No app, no signal needed.

The phone line is the collection mechanism. **The question stream is the product.**

Deliverables are live alerts, running summaries, and a post-event Crowd Question Intelligence Report. Call volume is usage. The confusion clusters (wayfinding, lineup changes, accessibility, gate policy) are the strategy.

Per-event packages for organizers. Free for attendees.

## Hard to shortcut by design

Staying intelligible over a phone line while a stage is peaking is the real engineering: background noise, turn detection, and uptime under load. This is an infrastructure problem, not a prompt wrapper.

## Stack

- **Live telephony.** A real inbound number per event. The full loop runs on phone infrastructure, not a browser demo.
- **Voice.** Deepgram for speech-to-text and Cartesia for text-to-speech, both via startup programs. The voice layer is swappable in code, so the stack is not tied to one vendor or one price.
- **Cloudflare-first platform.** Call handling, transcripts, and audio are processed and stored for clustering, live alerts, and reporting.

Festivals are the wedge. Phone is the first channel, not the last. Booth can ingest SMS, WhatsApp, kiosks, and more into one live question and intelligence layer, with venues as the retention engine and adjacent verticals as the platform play.
